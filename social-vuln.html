Social Vulnerability

March 26, 2023

This is an idea that's been kicking around in my head a bit, so I decided to finally put my pen to the paper (or more appropriately, put my fingers to the keyboard) and formalize my thoughts on it.

Most people would probably agree that one of the largest ways that we signal closeness in a friendship is through vulnerability. And, typically, people likely think of emotional vulnerability: being willing to share thoughts that are fairly intimate.

But I think that fits within a larger schema of vulnerability. Specifically, social vulnerability.

Social vulnerability is a phenomenon I've noticed wherein we signal closeness through the breaking of social norms.

This philosophy explains a lot of social interactions. Making racist jokes with friends? Breaks social norms. Trauma-dumping? Breaks social norms. Double texting? Breaks social norms.

The same framework can extend to relationships as well. Definitely explains why, for example, people are willing to act overly cutesy in relationships. Acting the same way with friends would be strange, and thus the action signals a level of closeness above even your closest friends. The same logic applies to physical intimacy.

It's important to not rush into social vulnerability, though. By their very nature, these actions break social norms. Thus, by definition, they come off weirdly if not paired with appropriate closeness.
